# Spread Duration

The sensitivity of a bond's price to a change in its credit spread, measured in years. Longer-duration bonds experience larger price moves for the same spread change. For credit analysis, spread duration determines how much mark-to-market pain a given spread widening inflicts on a portfolio.

**Why it matters in this framework:** Spread duration determines the P&L impact of credit events; two bonds with the same spread change can have dramatically different dollar losses based on duration.
