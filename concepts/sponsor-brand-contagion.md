# Sponsor-Brand Contagion

The hypothesis that stress in one vehicle managed by an alternative asset manager transmits to sibling vehicles through reputational association, even when the underlying portfolios and structures are distinct. In BDC context: non-traded vehicle gating may pressure public BDC unsecured spreads via perceived sponsor weakness, despite structural immunity of public vehicles to redemption runs.

**Why it matters in this framework:** Separating genuine portfolio contagion from sponsor-brand noise is critical for identifying mispriced public BDC credit after non-traded stress events.
