# Non-Traded BDC Structure

A BDC that does not trade on a public exchange, offering shares continuously at NAV with periodic repurchase windows (quarterly tender offers or interval fund schedules). Unlike public BDCs where the market imposes valuation discipline, non-traded vehicles are structurally opaque — investors cannot exit except at the manager's discretion.

**Why it matters in this framework:** The non-traded wrapper creates a fundamentally different risk profile from public BDCs; stress analysis must account for gating risk that public BDCs are structurally immune to.
