# Debt-Funded Buyback

When a company issues new debt specifically to finance share repurchases, leveraging the balance sheet to return capital to equity holders. The credit impact is a direct wealth transfer from bondholders (who bear increased leverage risk) to shareholders (who receive cash and per-share earnings accretion). Rating agencies treat this as a financial policy signal — it reveals management's willingness to prioritize equity returns over balance sheet conservatism.

**Why it matters in this framework:** This is the primary mechanism by which strong IG credits voluntarily weaken their credit profile, and the trigger for most financial-policy-driven rating actions.
