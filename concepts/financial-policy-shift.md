# Financial Policy Shift

A deliberate change in management's approach to balance sheet management — typically from conservative (low leverage, retained cash) to aggressive (debt-funded shareholder returns, tolerance for higher leverage). Rating agencies treat this as a forward-looking signal: the shift itself, not just the resulting leverage, triggers rating actions because it reveals management's willingness to prioritize equity holders over bondholders on an ongoing basis.

**Why it matters in this framework:** Financial policy shifts are the primary driver of voluntary credit deterioration in strong IG names; the shift signals a regime change, not a one-time event.
