# FLO — Flowers Foods Inc

US packaged bread and bakery producer, largest in category by market share; in coverage because an M&A-driven leverage event pushed it to the bottom of investment grade with all three rating agencies aligned in a negative direction, placing it on fallen angel watch.

## Business and fundamentals

Flowers Foods is the largest US packaged bread producer, operating brands including Nature's Own, Dave's Killer Bread, Canyon Bakehouse, and the newly acquired Simple Mills (snacking/crackers). The business is structurally low-growth: packaged bread volumes have been flat to declining for years, and FLO's revenue trajectory depends on pricing power, mix shift toward premium brands, and now Simple Mills integration. [from prior conversation — no raw source available]

Q1 2025 showed the pressure: sales of $1.554B declined 1.4% YoY. FY2025 guidance of $5.08-5.17B implies approximately -0.5% to +1.3% growth. FY26 EBITDA guidance is $465-495M. The base business faces consumer trade-down, promotional pressure from competitors, and tariff-related input cost increases. The Simple Mills acquisition was meant to add a higher-growth, higher-margin platform, but integration execution is unproven and the acquisition was financed entirely with debt. [from prior conversation — no raw source available]

## Rating cascade

The Simple Mills acquisition triggered a coordinated negative response from all three agencies. [from prior conversation — no raw source available]

**Fitch:** Downgraded from BBB to BBB- (stable outlook). FLO is now one notch from high yield at Fitch, the lowest rating among the three agencies. [from prior conversation — no raw source available]

**Moody's:** Downgraded from Baa2 to Baa3, outlook changed from stable to negative, governance assessment moved from G-1 to G-2. The governance downgrade reflects Moody's view that the M&A decision was aggressive relative to the issuer's historical financial policy. Deleveraging expectation is 12-18 months (mid-2026). [from prior conversation — no raw source available]

**S&P:** Affirmed BBB but moved outlook from stable to negative. Deleveraging expectation is adjusted leverage below 3.0x within 24 months (approximately Feb 2027). [from prior conversation — no raw source available]

All three agencies are aligned in a negative direction. This is a [[concepts/fallen-angel-framework]] case: the issuer is one notch from crossover at the lowest-rated agency, with negative momentum at the other two. [from prior conversation — no raw source available]

## Deleveraging deadlines

The agencies have set specific timelines for FLO to demonstrate deleveraging back toward pre-acquisition levels. [from prior conversation — no raw source available]

- **S&P:** Adjusted leverage below 3.0x within 24 months of acquisition close (approximately Feb 2027). Failure triggers potential downgrade to BBB-. [from prior conversation — no raw source available]
- **Moody's:** Expects deleveraging within 12-18 months (approximately mid-2026). This is the tightest deadline. Failure to show progress by mid-2026 could trigger downgrade to Ba1. [from prior conversation — no raw source available]
- **Fitch:** Already at BBB- stable. No explicit deleveraging deadline, but further deterioration in credit metrics would result in a downgrade to BB+. [from prior conversation — no raw source available]

The Moody's timeline is the binding constraint. FLO needs to demonstrate meaningful EBITDA growth and debt paydown by mid-2026 to avoid a Moody's downgrade, which would make it a split-rated fallen angel. [from prior conversation — no raw source available]

## Downgrade triggers (ranked by probability)

1. **EBITDA miss vs. FY26 guidance.** If FY26 EBITDA comes in below the $465-495M guidance range, leverage stays at 3.7x or higher, and a Moody's downgrade becomes near-certain. This is the highest-probability trigger because the base business is decelerating and guidance already embeds Simple Mills synergies. [from prior conversation — no raw source available]
2. **Botched Oct 2026 refi of the 3.50% 2026s.** Rolling $400M from 3.50% into 6%+ raises annual interest expense by approximately $10-12M and narrows the deleveraging math. If the refi is poorly timed or poorly executed, it signals to agencies that the capital structure is under stress. [from prior conversation — no raw source available]
3. **Simple Mills integration underperformance.** If Simple Mills fails to deliver the growth and margin profile that justified the acquisition premium, the strategic rationale weakens and the debt taken on to finance it looks worse. [from prior conversation — no raw source available]
4. **Additional M&A.** Any further debt-funded acquisition before leverage is back below 3.0x would likely trigger immediate multi-notch downgrades. [from prior conversation — no raw source available]

## Spread dynamics

FLO's positioning on the fallen angel watchlist creates specific [[concepts/spread-duration]] and liquidity dynamics. [from prior conversation — no raw source available]

IG-mandated buyers begin trimming exposure ahead of an actual downgrade as the [[concepts/rating-migration]] risk becomes priced in. This pre-downgrade selling widens spreads before the event and reduces secondary liquidity. [from prior conversation — no raw source available]

Index exclusion math is relevant for the 2035s and 2055s: if FLO falls out of IG indices, passive and benchmark-constrained accounts are forced sellers, creating a technical overhang. The crossover buyer base (high yield funds that buy recent fallen angels) has not yet engaged, meaning liquidity is likely to get worse before it gets better during any transition period.
