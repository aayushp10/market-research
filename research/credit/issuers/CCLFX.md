## Fund structure

CCLFX operates as a registered interval fund under the Investment Company Act of 1940. This structural choice is analytically significant relative to [[concepts/non-traded-bdc-structure]] peers.

**Interval fund mechanics:** Pre-determined quarterly repurchase intervals with a regulatory-maximum cap (CCLFX uses the 40 Act maximum of ~25% annually, 5-7% per quarter). The manager has no discretion to deny redemptions below the cap — if shares are tendered up to 7%, they must be honored. When requests exceed the cap, pro-rata allocation applies. [from prior conversation — no raw source available]

**Contrast with non-traded BDCs (PIMCO framing):** Interval funds eliminate manager discretion on redemption windows — the schedule and cap are pre-determined and regulatory. Non-traded BDCs leave redemption entirely at manager discretion, meaning investors are effectively short a put on their own liquidity. This is the core structural distinction between CCLFX and vehicles like BCRED or OBDC II. [from prior conversation — no raw source available]
