# CRM — Salesforce, Inc.

> The dominant enterprise CRM and cloud platform provider, recently thrust into credit focus by a record $25B bond offering to fund an aggressive $50B share repurchase program — a material financial policy shift that prompted a Moody's downgrade and S&P negative outlook.

## Snapshot

*Updated 2026-04-06.*

- **Ratings:** A2 (Moody's, downgraded from A1 Mar 2026) / A+ negative outlook (S&P). [[20260310-crm-ratings-moodys-sp|Rating action detail]].
- **Key spread levels:** 10yr tranche (2036, 5.550%) priced at ~135bps over Treasuries; 40yr tranche (2066, 6.700%) at ~185bps over. New issue tightening was 10bps vs typical 30bps — weak demand signal. [[20260313-crm-bond-demand-dynamics|Demand detail]].
- **Cap structure snapshot:** ~$33.5B pro forma total debt post-March 2026 issuance ($25B new senior notes + ~$8.5B legacy bonds; $6B term loan replaces existing facilities). ~$7.3B cash (pre-ASR deployment). Pro forma gross leverage ~2.0–2.1x; net leverage ~1.6–1.7x (using FY2026 OCF proxy for EBITDA). Pre-deal leverage was ~0.6x per S&P. [[20260313-crm-25b-bond-offering-8k|Full tranche detail]].
- **Fundamentals:** FY2026 revenue $41.5B (+10% YoY), FCF $14.4B (+16%), non-GAAP operating margin 34.1%. RPO $72.4B. Agentforce ARR $800M (+169%). [[20260225-crm-q4-fy2026-earnings|Earnings detail]].
- **Relative value:** Post-deal leverage makes CRM the second most levered name in enterprise software IG (behind ORCL at 14.2x), but still moderate at ~2x. Front-end bonds (2028–2031) reasonably attractive; back-end (2046–2066) carries AI disruption risk premium that may be insufficient.
- **Catalyst calendar:** FY2027 Q1 earnings (~late May 2026); ASR final settlement Q4 2026; potential further buyback debt if management continues current financial policy trajectory.

## Business and fundamentals

Salesforce is the world's largest CRM platform and the dominant enterprise cloud application vendor by revenue. The company operates a multi-cloud SaaS platform spanning Sales Cloud, Service Cloud, Marketing Cloud, Commerce Cloud, Platform (including Heroku), and more recently Data Cloud and Agentforce (agentic AI). Revenue is overwhelmingly subscription-based (~94%), providing high visibility and predictability. The Informatica acquisition (closed FY2026, contributing $399M in Q4 alone) extends CRM's data integration and management capabilities.

**Key financial characteristics:**

- **Revenue scale and growth:** $41.5B FY2026 (+10%), with FY2027 guidance of $45.8–$46.2B (+10–11%). Long-term target of $63B by FY2030. Growth is durable but moderating from the hypergrowth phase.
- **Margin expansion story:** GAAP operating margin 20.1% (vs low teens a few years ago); non-GAAP 34.1%. Management has successfully pivoted from growth-at-all-costs to profitable growth under pressure from activist investors (Starboard, Elliott, ValueAct in 2023).
- **Cash generation:** FCF of $14.4B (34.7% FCF margin) is exceptional. This is the fundamental credit strength — CRM prints cash at a rate that makes even substantial debt loads manageable.
- **RPO backlog:** $72.4B provides ~1.7 years of revenue visibility. cRPO of $35.1B (+16%) indicates near-term demand remains strong.
- **AI pivot:** Agentforce (agentic AI) is the strategic response to AI disruption concerns. ARR of $800M growing 169% is encouraging but still small relative to the $41.5B revenue base (~2%).

## Sector context

CRM sits in Enterprise Software within the Technology mega-sector. IG tech credit is a crowded and generally tight-trading space, with most large-cap tech names carrying minimal leverage. CRM was historically in this camp — near-zero net leverage, strong cash generation, occasional M&A-driven leverage spikes that were rapidly delevered.

The March 2026 financial policy shift changes CRM's position in the sector:

- **vs MSFT (Aaa/AAA):** Microsoft is the gold standard, net cash, trades at the tightest spreads in tech. CRM is no longer comparable.
- **vs ORCL (A3/BBB+):** Oracle is the other end of the spectrum — 14.2x net debt/EBITDA, serial debt-funded acquirer, aggressively levered for AI infrastructure. CRM is nowhere near ORCL's leverage, but the *direction* of financial policy is now ORCL-ward.
- **vs ADBE (A3/A):** Adobe is roughly neutral leverage, conservative financial policy. CRM now trades wider.
- **vs SAP (A2):** Comparable rating, but SAP has lower leverage and a more conservative financial policy.

CRM is now the highest-leveraged A-rated enterprise software credit, a distinction it did not hold before March 2026. The market is treating it accordingly — wider spreads than the rating alone would suggest.

**Overall Quality Tier: Strategic**

Average score: 1.8. Two sub-factors at 1 (cash flow, competitive position), three at 2, one at 3 (event risk). No sub-factor at 4, so no automatic override. The credit is fundamentally strong — exceptional cash generation, dominant market position, manageable leverage even post-deal. But the event risk score of 3 (driven by financial policy) prevents a Core rating and is the key monitorable.

### Lens 2: Relative Value Opinion

**Peer set:** MSFT, ORCL, ADBE, SAP, INTU (IG enterprise software)

**Current spreads (as of March 2026 new issue):**
- CRM 10yr (2036, 5.550%): ~135bps over Treasuries
- CRM 40yr (2066, 6.700%): ~185bps over Treasuries
- Demand was weak: 1.4x coverage (vs Amazon's concurrent 3.4x)
- Front-end tightened more; back-end barely moved from initial price talk

**Spread drivers:**
- Financial policy premium: market is charging for the leveraging event
- AI disruption premium: long-dated bonds especially penalized by existential AI uncertainty
- Supply overhang: $25B is a massive slug of new paper that needs to be digested
- Liquidity premium: new issue bonds are liquid but the sheer volume creates technical pressure

**RV Opinion: Market Perform**

Spreads are approximately fair for the current risk profile. The front-end (2028–2031) is more interesting — shorter duration limits AI disruption exposure, and CRM's cash flows can comfortably handle the near-term maturities. The back-end (2046–2066) carries risk that the spread may not fully compensate for: 20–40 year exposure to a company whose core product (traditional CRM workflows) faces genuine AI disruption questions. The 1.85% spread on the 40yr tranche (185bps) is not obviously cheap for that risk.

**Divergence:** Fundamentals Strategic + RV Market Perform → No compelling alpha. Carry is acceptable. Monitor for further financial policy shifts (which would widen) or AI strategy execution proof points (which could tighten).

## Open questions

- **Financial policy trajectory:** Is the $50B buyback the ceiling, or will management continue to lever the balance sheet? The remaining $25B unfunded portion of the buyback could require additional debt.
- **S&P downgrade timing:** With negative outlook, how long before S&P acts? A downgrade from A+ to A would put CRM at A/A2 — still solidly IG but a meaningful step down from the A+/A1 profile of 12 months ago.
- **AI disruption materiality:** Agentforce is growing fast ($800M ARR) but remains ~2% of revenue. Is this enough to defend the franchise if hyperscaler AI agents genuinely displace traditional CRM workflows? The 40-year bondholders are making a bet on this.
- **Acquisition risk:** CRM is a serial acquirer (Slack, Informatica). Could another large debt-funded deal push leverage further? The current financial policy posture suggests management would not rule it out.
- **FCF trajectory post-deal:** With ~$1.8B in incremental annual interest expense, will FCF still grow? FY2026 FCF was $14.4B — even after interest, coverage is ample, but the margin of safety is smaller than before.

## Confidence and what would change my mind

**Confidence: Medium-High on fundamental quality, Medium on RV call.**

The fundamental assessment (Strategic tier) is high-conviction: CRM generates $14B+ FCF annually, has a dominant market position, and even at ~2x leverage is comfortably investment grade. The cash flow engine is exceptional and well-proven.

The RV assessment (Market Perform) is more uncertain because:
- Spreads on newly issued paper are still settling; secondary trading levels will reveal true clearing spread
- The AI disruption narrative is evolving rapidly — if Agentforce gains traction, spreads tighten; if hyperscaler AI agents erode CRM's moat, spreads widen materially, especially on long-dated paper
- Financial policy is the wild card — if management issues another $10–15B for the remaining buyback, leverage approaches 3x and a further downgrade becomes likely

**What would change this to Outperform (tighter call):**
- CRM demonstrably de-levers to <1.5x within 18 months via FCF application
- Agentforce reaches $3B+ ARR, proving AI is additive not cannibalistic
- S&P revises outlook back to stable
- Spreads widen another 20–30bps on technicals (supply digestion) creating a better entry

**What would change this to Underperform (wider call):**
- Management announces additional debt-funded buybacks pushing leverage >3x
- S&P downgrades to A; Moody's downgrades to A3 (double-notch from pre-deal)
- Agentforce growth stalls or hyperscaler AI agents demonstrate clear CRM substitution
- Enterprise spending downturn compresses FCF below $10B
- Another large debt-funded acquisition
