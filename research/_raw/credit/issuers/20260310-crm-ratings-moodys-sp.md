# Salesforce Rating Actions (March 2026)

## Moody's

- **Action:** Downgrade
- **Prior rating:** A1
- **New rating:** A2 (stable outlook)
- **Rationale:** Cited "a material shift in financial policy, including a higher tolerance for debt in the capital structure" driven by the debt-funded $50B buyback program. Highlighted leverage and potential balance sheet risks.

## S&P Global Ratings

- **Action:** Outlook revision
- **Rating maintained:** A+
- **Outlook:** Changed to Negative (from Stable)
- **Rationale:** S&P noted CRM "still has capacity for major buybacks" but expects leverage to increase. Cited debt-to-EBITDA at ~0.6x as of January 31, 2026 (pre-deal), with room for deterioration.

## Key Themes from Both Agencies

1. Financial policy shift — using debt to fund shareholder returns rather than organic growth investment
2. Concern about higher sustained leverage vs prior near-zero net debt profile
3. Both agencies acknowledged CRM's strong underlying business and cash generation
4. Uncertainty about AI's impact on traditional enterprise software business models
