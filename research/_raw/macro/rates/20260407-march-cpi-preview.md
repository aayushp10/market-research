# March 2026 CPI Preview

## Release Date

**Friday, April 10, 2026, 8:30 AM ET.** (BLS schedule)

## Consensus Forecasts

- **Headline CPI m/m:** 0.9% — sharpest one-month advance since 2022 (Bloomberg, Econbrowser)
- **Headline CPI y/y:** 3.3% (Bloomberg)
- **Cleveland Fed Nowcast:** 3.16% y/y trailing 12-month (up from 2.4% in February) (Cleveland Fed)

## Gasoline Pass-Through

Gasoline prices jumped ~35% from the Iran war. Estimated to add 0.5–0.6% to CPI. Fuel costs filter into transportation, shipping, and consumer goods prices with a lag. (Truflation, Bloomberg)

Economists pencil in a 1% increase in CPI for March — the "sharpest one-month advance since 2022" — after the Iran war pushed gas prices at the pump up ~$1/gallon. (Bloomberg)

## Implications

A hot CPI could force a major market repricing. If headline prints >3.5%, the "Fed on hold through 2026" theme firms up decisively. Post-CPI is also the test for whether IG primary re-opens — supply has been light, which is supporting the technical bid. (Seeking Alpha, Econbrowser)
