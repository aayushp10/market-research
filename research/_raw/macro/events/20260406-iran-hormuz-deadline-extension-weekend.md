# Iran War Weekend Developments — April 6, 2026

## Hormuz Deadline Extended 24 Hours (3rd Extension)

Trump extended deadline for Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz by an additional 24 hours — new deadline **Tuesday 8 PM ET**. This is the third time Trump has pushed the ultimatum. Trump issued a profanity-laced social media threat Sunday: "Open the Fuckin' Strait" or face strikes on bridges and power plants. (Times of Israel, Al Jazeera, CNBC)

## Iran's 10-Point Counter-Proposal

Iran formally rejected the US/Pakistan-brokered ceasefire and delivered a 10-point counter-proposal via Pakistan. Key demands: permanent end to hostilities (not temporary ceasefire), safe passage protocol for the Strait of Hormuz, reconstruction commitments, lifting of sanctions. Iran stated it would not reopen Hormuz for anything less than a permanent ceasefire. (CNN, Tribune India, i24 News, Xinhua)

Trump called Iran's proposal "a significant step" but said it was "not good enough" at the White House Easter Egg Roll. (CNBC, NBC News)

## Israel Re-Strikes South Pars

Israel struck Iran's South Pars petrochemical plant for the **second time** — after Netanyahu previously pledged to honor Trump's instruction not to re-strike the facility following the March 18 raid. Israeli Defense Minister Katz called it a strike on "the largest petrochemical facility in Iran" responsible for half of Iran's petrochemical production. (Euronews, PBS, Military.com)

## IRGC Commanders Killed

Two senior IRGC commanders killed: IRGC intelligence chief **Maj. Gen. Majid Khademi** and Quds Force undercover unit leader **Asghar Bakeri**. (Legal Insurrection, Washington Times, PBS)

## Civilian Casualties

At least 19 people killed, 20+ injured in US and Israeli airstrikes near Tehran. Districts of Shahriar and Baharestan hit; two residential buildings destroyed. (NBC News, CNN)

## Mediator Track

The 45-day ceasefire proposal from Egypt/Pakistan/Turkey mediators remains on the table. Neither side has formally accepted. Pakistan proposed a two-phase plan: immediate ceasefire followed by 15-20 days of negotiations for broader settlement including Hormuz governance framework. (Al Jazeera, CBS News, CNBC)
