# CRM Bond Sale — Yield Demand and Pricing (March 2026)

## Spread and Pricing

- 10-year tranche (2036, 5.550%): priced at ~135bps over Treasuries
- Spread was noticeably wider than CRM's 2021 Slack-acquisition debt offering
- Characterized as "cautious reception" from investors

## Demand Dynamics

- Investor appetite weaker than expected
- Buyers demanded higher yields than previous CRM debt offerings
- Institutional participants (pension funds, insurers, asset managers) still buying for yield
- Deal characterized as "a maturing corporate playbook" of leveraging debt for equity enhancement

## Underwriters

- J.P. Morgan Securities (joint book-running manager)
- Bank of America Securities (joint book-running manager)
- Barclays Capital, Citigroup Global Markets, Wells Fargo Securities

## Key Investor Concerns

1. Leverage increase specifically for equity buyback programs (not investment)
2. AI uncertainty's long-term impact on enterprise software growth
3. Duration risk on long-dated tranches given AI disruption timeline
