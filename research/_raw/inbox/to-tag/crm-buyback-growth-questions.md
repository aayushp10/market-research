# CRM Buyback Growth Story Questions — March 2026

## Core Thesis

The $50B buyback program raises questions about whether Salesforce's growth story is maturing. The buyback functions as "a safety valve for excess capital if organic growth slows."

## Financial Data Points

- Q4 FY2026 revenue: $11.2B (beat $11.18B consensus), +12% YoY (fastest in two years)
- FY2027 guidance: $45.8–$46.2B (10–11% growth)
- LSEG consensus was $46.06B — guidance just below
- FY2026 operating cash flow: ~$15B projected
- Q3 FY2026 non-GAAP operating margin: 35.5%
- $4.2B returned to shareholders in Q3 FY2026 alone

## Market Reaction

- Post-earnings stock decline of ~3% despite beat — "sell the news" dynamic
- Growth deceleration from 12% to 10–11% guidance overwhelmed positive results
- CRM stock down ~30% YTD as of March 2026

## Analyst Assessment

- Average analyst price target: $313 (implying significant upside from ~$185)
- Credibility depends on whether guidance reset is temporary pause or permanent trajectory shift
- Maintaining >30% non-GAAP margins "is no longer optional — it's a necessity to fund both the buyback and AI investments"

## Growth Visibility

- RPO: $60B (+12% growth) — described as "stable base, not a growth catalyst"
- cRPO constant-currency growth: 11% YoY (Q3)
- 60%+ of Agentforce/Data 360 Q4 bookings from existing customer expansion

## AI Disruption Framing

Stock decline driven by market fears that "AI tools could render traditional CRM functions obsolete." Salesforce must "defend its legacy and monetize its AI platform without sacrificing profitability."

## Key Monitoring Metrics (per article)

1. cRPO consistency — acceleration challenges the new growth path
2. AI deal velocity — signals competitive positioning
3. Margin trajectory — tests sustainability of capital return program
4. Analyst revisions — continued downgrades indicate deeper skepticism
