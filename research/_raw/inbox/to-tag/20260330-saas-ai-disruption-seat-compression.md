# SaaS AI Disruption — "SaaSpocalypse" Analysis (March 2026)

## Market-Wide Impact

- SaaS industry lost >$1T in market capitalization in early 2026
- iShares Expanded Tech-Software ETF (IGV): ~20% below 200-day MA — widest gap since 2000 dot-com crash
- Sector rotation away from software into value and industrials

## The "Seat Compression" Dynamic

Core thesis: AI agents replace human workers, reducing per-seat software licenses needed. "If an AI agent can perform the work of five human employees, a company only needs to pay for one software license instead of five." This renders traditional per-seat licensing models vulnerable.

| Company | Decline | Specific Impact |

| Intuit (INTU) | -46% | AI-driven tax/bookkeeping automation |
| Workday (WDAY) | -40% | Seat compression from reduced headcount |
| Snowflake (SNOW) | -37% | AI models bypass specialized platforms |
| Adobe (ADBE) | -36% | AI-native competitors (Canva, Midjourney) |
| Atlassian (TEAM) | -35% | First systemic decline in enterprise seats |
| Salesforce (CRM) | -33% | Fear of AI replicating CRM workflows |

## Winners vs Losers

- Winners: NVIDIA, Palantir (AI infrastructure/hardware)
- Performance gap vs legacy software: 95 percentage points over 12 months
- Losers: Traditional enterprise software vendors with seat-based models

## Structural Shifts

- MVP development costs collapsed from $500K to $20K — enables lean AI-native competitors
- Legacy firms must transition from per-seat to outcome-based or usage-based pricing
- Transition causes margin compression during pivot period

## Hyperscaler Capital Spend Context

Big Five hyperscalers plan $660–690B infrastructure spend in 2026 (~2x 2025 levels), with ~75% ($450B+) targeting AI infrastructure. This directly competes with enterprise software for corporate IT budgets.

## S&P Credit Perspective (per separate S&P report)

- AI impacts software companies case-by-case, not sector-wide credit rating declines
- Firms with 2027–2028 debt maturities and rule-based products face highest risk
- "Today's environment reflects structural technological evolution rather than an abrupt macroeconomic shock"
- Proprietary data and deep integration provide defensive moats
