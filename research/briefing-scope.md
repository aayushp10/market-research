This file tells the agent what's in scope for morning briefings. The agent reads this first every morning. Aayush edits it directly to add/remove coverage or re-weight themes. The agent proposes amendments at the bottom of daily reviews when Aayush's corrections reveal scope changes.

## Macro factors in scope

Always track these daily where relevant:
- **Rates**: UST curve shape, 10y level, Fed expectations, CPI/PCE prints
- **Oil/energy**: WTI level, Iran war developments, Hormuz transit status, gasoline prices (pass-through to CPI)
- **Credit technicals**: CDX IG, CDX HY, new issue calendar, ETF flows
- **Credit fundamentals**: dispersion within IG sectors, HY distress ratio
- **Equity vol**: VIX level, cross-asset vol regime
- **DXY/FX**: dollar strength, debasement bid (gold, crypto, long equities)

## Sector sensitivities to track

- **Energy IG sub-industries**: decompose by Integrated / E&P / Refiners / Gas Pipes / Chems / Airlines whenever WTI moves >5% day or >15% week. Never report "Energy IG" as a single read during an oil move.
- **Banks ↔ curve shape**: steeper curve is NIM tailwind, flatter is headwind. Fed path matters.
- **Insurance ↔ private credit**: life insurers with heavy private credit allocations are the transmission channel from BDC stress into public IG.
- **Tech IG ↔ AI capex**: hyperscaler capex cycle drives ORCL and tech mega-caps. Software weakness is a DIFFERENT signal and affects tech-BDCs, not hyperscalers.
- **Consumer goods ↔ input costs**: FLO, KDP and food producers sensitive to grain, energy, packaging costs.

## Active themes (cycle-level)

These are multi-week themes Aayush is watching. Daily briefings should report material developments on any of these:

1. **Iran war → energy → credit transmission** (since 2026-02-28). Currently: Week 5/6, oil +94% YTD, Hormuz partially blocked, US invasion odds elevated. Priority: very high. Sub-industry decomposition required.
2. **Non-traded BDC redemption stress → public BDC unsecured contagion** (acute 2026-04-02 Blue Owl prints). Currently: active setup. Priority: very high for OBDC/OTF, and for the life insurers transmission channel.
3. **IG credit rally durability vs weak macro**. Currently: MTD rally narrow (banks, tech, healthcare), off the March wides not to tights, light issuance doing the work. Priority: high. Key test on primary re-opening and CPI print.
4. **Fed on hold through 2026 / stagflation-lite**. Currently: 99.5% no-cut April, 78% no-cut year-end. Priority: medium. Inflection point would be a material CPI surprise.
5. **Software/AI exposure as BDC risk factor**. Currently: specific to tech-focused BDCs (OTIC 40.7% redemptions). Distinct from hyperscaler AI capex which is separate. Priority: medium, watch for spillover.

## What's OUT of scope (intentionally)

Don't report on these unless they're directly relevant to the themes above:

- Broad equity market commentary (unless credit-relevant)
- EM credit (unless a specific sovereign or name enters active coverage)
- Muni credit
- Crypto (except as macro debasement bid proxy)
- Generic Fed-speak rehash without new information
- European credit (unless there's a specific US read-through)
- Commodity supercycle narratives (unless oil-specific and WTI is moving)
